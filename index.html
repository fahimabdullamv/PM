Case Studies

01 · Consumer Feed

Improving D7 Retention Through Personalised Feed Ranking

A large portion of new users dropped off by day 7 — not because the content was bad, but because they saw the same types of posts repeatedly. The feed wasn't learning fast enough from early signals. High scroll rate, low save/share rate: users were bored, not engaged.

"The real problem wasn't content quality. It was that the ranking model was optimising for short-term clicks, not the type of engagement that predicts long-term retention."

My Role
Problem Definition User Research Metrics Framework Stakeholder Alignment PRD & Spec
Approach
  • Conducted 18 user interviews across new and churned users to understand content fatigue patterns
  • Analysed D1/D3/D7 retention cohorts with SQL to isolate the exact drop-off point and content categories driving churn
  • Defined new North Star metric: Saves + Shares per session (predictive of week-2 return) instead of raw session time
  • Wrote PRD for a diversity-injection layer in the feed ranking pipeline, worked with engineers on signal weighting
  • A/B tested with 10% traffic, iterated on diversity thresholds, rolled out in 3 phases
Outcomes
+18% D7 Retention
+31% Save Rate
−22% Churn D1–D7
Key Learning
  • Optimising for the wrong metric at launch creates engagement debt that's expensive to recover from
  • Diversity in feed isn't about randomness — it's about pace of learning user preference signals
02 · Creator Tools

Reducing Creator Activation Drop-off by 40%

Creators were signing up but fewer than 20% published their first piece of content within 7 days. A bloated, intimidating onboarding flow — designed for power users — was crushing activation for new creators who needed quick wins to build confidence.

"First-time creators don't need all the tools — they need one successful moment. Every step before that first publish is a potential exit point."

My Role
Discovery Research Journey Mapping Hypothesis Testing Eng Collaboration Launch & Iteration
Approach
  • Mapped full creator journey using session recordings and drop-off funnels — identified that step 3 of 7 had 60% abandonment
  • Ran 12 user interviews with non-activated creators; discovered the permission request flow felt "like a government form"
  • Proposed progressive onboarding: remove all non-critical steps from first session, defer power features to post-first-publish
  • Coordinated with the Design and Engineering squads to ship an MVP in 2 sprints — simplified 7-step flow to 3 steps
  • Set up instrumentation for first-publish funnel before rollout to measure with precision
Outcomes
+40% Activation Rate
−58% Onboard Time
2x W1 Publish Rate
Key Learning
  • Complexity in onboarding is usually a symptom of internal org structure, not user needs
  • The best onboarding is invisible — it's just the product working as users expect it to
03 · Pricing & Conversion

Redesigning Value Communication to Drive Purchase Intent

Cart abandonment was high despite aggressive discounts. Analytics showed users were adding items but bailing at the price confirmation screen. Exit survey data pointed to a single insight: users didn't trust the displayed savings — the discount UI felt confusing and potentially deceptive.

"The discount was real. The problem was that users couldn't parse it. Perception of value is a design problem as much as a pricing problem."

My Role
Problem Framing Exit Survey Analysis UX Strategy A/B Testing Conversion Tracking
Approach
  • Synthesised exit survey data with heatmap analysis to pinpoint the exact UI elements eroding trust
  • Ran a heuristic audit of the pricing display against 6 regional competitor apps — identified dark pattern proximity as the trust erosion driver
  • Defined 3 hypotheses for price display clarity, designed test variants with the Design team for controlled A/B test
  • Worked with the Data team to set up funnel tracking from PDP → Cart → Checkout confirmation as a single cohorted flow
  • Shipped winning variant; led post-launch review at 14-day and 30-day marks to validate durability of lift
Outcomes
+24% Checkout Conv.
−19% Cart Abandon
+11% Repeat Purchase
Key Learning
  • Trust is harder to build and easier to lose than conversion rate optimization frameworks suggest
  • Price perception is psychological — you can't discount your way out of a trust deficit
